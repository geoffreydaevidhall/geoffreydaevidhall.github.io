I am currently an Ontological Engineer at Cycorp, where I work on using higher-order logic to formalize common sense heuristics and inference patterns in addition to working on automated natural language processing. In August 2020 I received a PhD in philosophy from the University of Notre Dame. My dissertation, The Algebraic Structure of Propositions, applied some general algebraic tools to questions about propositions and propositional attitudes. My dissertation was co-advised by Jeff Speaks and Daniel Nolan. Before coming to Notre Dame I completed a B.A. in philosophy at Lewis & Clark College.

In philosophy, my primary research interests are in the philosophy of mind and metaphysics, with a particular focus on the the metaphysics of content and the metaphysics of conscious experience. I have additional interests in many areas of philosophy, however. Some other recent topics I have thought about are the principle of sufficient reason (Indefinite Extensibility and the Principle of Sufficient Reason), the problem of the unity of the proposition (Unity and Application) and the the epistemic value of naturalness (Naturalness is Not an Aim of Belief) among other things.
