I am currently a adjunct professor in the philosophy department at the University of Notre Dame. I also work as an Ontological Engineer at Cycorp, focusing on artificial intelligence and formalized reasoning. In August of 2020 I defended a dissertation entitled The Algebraic Structure of Propositions, and graduated with my Ph.D. from the University of Notre Dame. My dissertation was co-advised by Jeff Speaks and Daniel Nolan. Before coming to Notre Dame I completed a B.A. in philosophy at Lewis & Clark College.

My primary research interests are in the philosophy of mind and metaphysics, with a particular focus on the the metaphysics of content. I have additional interests in many areas of philosophy, however. Some other recent topics I have thought about are the principle of sufficient reason (Indefinite Extensibility and the Principle of Sufficient Reason), the problem of the unity of the proposition (Unity and Application) and the the epistemic value of naturalness (Naturalness is Not an Aim of Belief) among other things.

Outside of philosophy, I enjoy watching movies, dabbling in maths, biking and (occasionally) skateboarding. You can view my CV here. Some further information can also be found on my Phil People profile.
