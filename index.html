I am currently a postdoctoral fellow in the philosophy department at the University of Notre Dame. I have recently defended a dissertation entitled The Algebraic Structure of Propositions, and graduated with my Ph.D. from the University of Notre Dame in August 2020. I was co-advised by Jeff Speaks and Daniel Nolan. Before coming to Notre Dame I completed a B.A. in philosophy at Lewis & Clark College.

My primary research interests are in metaphysics and the philosophy of mind with particular focus on the metaphysics of propositions, properties and relations, in metaphysics, and phenomenal consciousness in philosophy of mind. I have additional interests in many areas of philosophy, however. Some other recent topics I have thought about are the principle of sufficient reason (Indefinite Extensibility and the Principle of Sufficient Reason), the problem of the unity of the proposition (Unity and Application) and the the epistemic value of naturalness.
